The Art of Mindful Reading

By Shiv Kumar • 4 min read • Design & Layout

In an era dominated by rapid notifications and endless scrolling, the ability to focus on a single piece of text has become a rare skill. Mindfulness in reading is not just about speed; it is about retention and deep connection with the author's underlying ideas. When we slow down, the words take on a physical weight.

Highlighters and markers have long served as our guides through dense texts. By marking key passages, we map out cognitive landmarks that help our brains catalog insights. In digital mediums, we can capture this tactile joy through fluid, smoothly drawn highlight transitions that mimic the ink sweeping across paper.

We can also assign colors to signify different themes. For instance, we might use a green highlight for definitions, a cyan highlight for citations, or a pink highlight for warnings and critical notes. This keeps our annotations structured, readable, and highly referenceable.
